For data processing, the authors used tweet datasets from COVID and the 2020 election. They removed inactive users and users not from the US. To remove biases from bots, they used BotOrNot, which scores the likelyhood of the user being a bot from from 0 to 1 using metadata and other features. They took the top 10% of users based on bot scores and removed them from the dataset.

For seed user labeling, they had a heuristic-based pseudo labeling method. Using hashtags, they classified users based on different hashtags in their tweets, with 17 left-leaning hashtags and 12 right-leaning hashtags
For the news media-based methods, they referenced 29 media outlets that are scored on a scale of 1 to 5 on AllSides.com based on political leaning, where 1 is left, 3 is center and 5 is right.For a user who has given at least two endorsements to any of these media, their media bias score is computed as the average of the scores of their media outlets. A user is considered left-leaning if their media bias score is equal to or below 2 and right-leaning if their score is equal or above 4.
