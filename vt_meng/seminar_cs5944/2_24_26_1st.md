# Richard Li

This seminar centers on the development of patient-centered healthcare technologies, focusing on systems like Beacon, a cognitive monitoring tool shown to correlate strongly with clinical measures such as BFF. The speaker highlights both the feasibility and challenges of deploying such systems in real-world settings, including hardware limitations and app distribution setbacks. A key theme is empowering patients through self-measurement and data-driven care, particularly in liver disease management, where both patients and clinicians see value in tools like CFF for improving autonomy and treatment adjustments.

The seminar also explores bridging the gap between clinical evidence and real-world practice, especially in exercise adherence for postoperative recovery (e.g., bladder cancer patients). While clinicians anticipate low adherence, solutions such as app-based exercise prescriptions, real-time computer vision monitoring, and integration with health systems (like Epic) aim to improve compliance. Technical constraints in deploying computer vision on mobile devices are addressed through lightweight modeling approaches, such as 2D keypoint extraction and data augmentation.

Results from a study show improved adherence rates (46% vs. a 30% baseline), demonstrating the value of objective monitoring. The seminar also introduces a generative pipeline for creating personalized exercise demonstrations using natural language, where an orchestrator agent modifies pose representations for interpretability and customization (e.g., avatars for patients with disabilities). The talk concludes with broader lessons on building healthcare software—prioritizing patient needs, scalability, and real-world deployment—and outlines future directions such as multimodal sensing and opportunistic health interventions.

I would recommend the speaker as a strong research contributor, particularly in interdisciplinary healthcare technology. Their work demonstrates a thoughtful balance between technical innovation and practical application, addressing both system-level challenges and patient outcomes. Their ability to connect machine learning, healthcare, and human-centered design makes their research impactful and relevant, though some areas (like technical depth in CV methods) could benefit from clearer simplification for broader audiences.
