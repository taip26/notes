Hashing
 - key-value
 - hash function denoted by h
 - HT is a hash table
 - HT has M slots indexed from 0 to M - 1
 - sometimes hashing isn't good
  - if keys have a lot of dups
  - can cause collisions
 - generally good for mem and disk app

example
 - in a student record
  - store PID, name, DoB, userid, department, GPA, classes, etc.
 - walked through a simple len(10) HT with keys in range 0-9
 - walked through another example but with keys in range 0-16384, not reasonable to make table of O(M)
 - explained overhead in the context of HT vs BSTs and linked lists (empty slots)

collisions
 - if they collide, need to consult a collision policy
 - a little redundant in explanation of collisions
 - hash function should distribute records with equal prob to all hash table slots
 - success depends on actual records stored

diff hash strats
 - simple mod - prioritize lsd
 - binning - prio most sig dig
 - normal distribution assumption is usually good
 - mid-square - calculates a hash based on the middle 2 digits of a 4 digit num
